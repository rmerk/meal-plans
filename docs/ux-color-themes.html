Based on your PRD requirements and user context, I recommend the Mountains at Sunrise (Primary) theme with the Dark Mode variant. Here's why:

  • Warm & Approachable: The earthy tones create the "trusted cooking companion" feeling described in your UX principles
  • Natural & Grounded: Earth tones align with food/cooking domain (terra cotta, clay, sunrise gold)
  • Professional Yet Friendly: Deep blue provides confidence while warm accents prevent coldness
  • Excellent Contrast: Meets WCAG AA standards while maintaining visual warmth
  • Dark Mode Ready: Natural palette adapts beautifully to dark mode (important for cooking at night)
  • Distinctive: Stands out from typical food app blues/greens while remaining approachable

The High Contrast theme can be offered as an optional accessibility mode for users with vision impairments, and the Minimal theme shows how the palette works with lots of whitespace (useful for certain pages like Settings).
