Order of Operations

Follow these 5 steps in order for the most efficient batch prep workflow. Check off each step as you complete it!

  1. 1

    1. Prep Carbs

    Start the rice (2 cups dry) and cook soba noodles. Rinse noodles with cold water & oil.

    üí° Pro Tip:

    Use a rice cooker on the "keep warm" setting while you prep everything else. For soba, rinse thoroughly with cold water to remove excess starch and prevent clumping.

  2. 2

    2. Vegetables & Aromatics

    Chop everything at once. Mince ~30 cloves garlic, grate ginger. Chop broccoli, cabbage, carrots, onions, bok choy.

    üí° Pro Tip:

    Use a food processor for cabbage and carrots to save 15 minutes. Store prepped garlic and ginger in small bowls covered with plastic wrap to prevent drying out.

  3. 3

    3. Mix Sauces

    Make all 4 sauces in separate jars/bowls so they are ready to dump in pan.

    üí° Pro Tip:

    Label each jar/bowl with the recipe name. Having sauces pre-mixed speeds up cooking dramatically and ensures consistent flavor across all portions.

  4. 4

    4. Cook & Assemble

    Cook one meal at a time. Portion immediately into containers. Note: Keep salad components separate.

    üí° Pro Tip:

    For the Vietnamese chicken salad, use 3 separate containers per serving: vegetables, chicken, and dressing. This keeps everything crisp for the full week!

  5. 5

    5. Cool & Store

    Let hot meals cool 30-45 mins before lidding to prevent sogginess.

    üí° Pro Tip:

    Place containers on a cooling rack for better air circulation. Once cool, stack in the fridge with oldest meals in front for easy "first in, first out" rotation.

Essential Prep Tips

Mise en Place

Have all ingredients measured and prepped before you start cooking. This makes the actual cooking process smooth and stress-free.

Time Management

While rice cooks and noodles boil, use that time to chop vegetables and mix sauces. Efficient multitasking cuts total time by 30-45 minutes.

Container Organization

Use glass containers when possible - they reheat better and don't stain. Label each with the meal name and date for easy tracking.

Food Safety

Never put hot food directly in the fridge - it raises the internal temperature. Always let meals cool to room temp first, then refrigerate within 2 hours.
