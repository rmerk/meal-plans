Pork & Soba Noodle Stir-Fry

Savory pork loin with chewy soba noodles, crisp baby bok choy, and colorful bell peppers

Ingredients

Scale:

Main Ingredients

  • 1.125 lbs pork loin (thinly sliced)
  • 6 oz dry soba noodles
  • 12-15 baby bok choy
  • 3 bell peppers (sliced)

Sauce

  • 9 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 3 tsp sesame oil
  • 3 tsp honey
  • 6 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 3 tsp fresh ginger (grated)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Cook soba noodles according to package directions (usually 5-7 minutes in boiling water). Drain and rinse thoroughly with cold water to stop cooking. Toss with a small drizzle of sesame oil to prevent sticking. Set aside.

  2. 2

    In a bowl or jar, whisk together all sauce ingredients: soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, honey, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Set aside.

  3. 3

    Heat a large wok or skillet over high heat. Add thinly sliced pork loin and stir-fry for 4-5 minutes until fully cooked and lightly browned. Remove pork and set aside.

  4. 4

    In the same wok, stir-fry baby bok choy and sliced bell peppers for 3-4 minutes until tender-crisp. The bok choy should be wilted but still bright green, and peppers should have slight char.

  5. 5

    Return the cooked pork to the wok. Add the cooked soba noodles and pour in the prepared sauce. Toss everything together vigorously for 2-3 minutes until hot, well combined, and the sauce coats all ingredients.

  6. 6

    Divide the stir-fry among 3 meal prep containers. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions if desired. This dish is delicious both hot and at room temperature!

Storage & Tips

Storage

Store in airtight containers in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. Soba noodles absorb some sauce over time, which actually makes the flavor even better on day 2-3!

Soba Noodle Tips

Always rinse soba noodles with cold water after cooking - this removes excess starch and gives them a better texture. Look for buckwheat soba for authentic flavor and extra nutrients!

Reheating

Microwave for 2-3 minutes, stirring halfway through. Add a splash of water if the noodles seem dry. For best results, reheat in a skillet with a tablespoon of water to steam and refresh the dish.
