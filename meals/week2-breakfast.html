Week 2: High-Protein Breakfasts

~400-450 Calories • Ready in 10 Minutes

Week 2 Strategy

Same as Week 1: these recipes are designed to be made fresh in under 10 minutes. The goal is to start the day with 30-40g of protein to keep you full until lunch.

  • ✓ Variety: Four new flavors to prevent boredom.
  • ✓ Rotation: Pick 1-2 favorites for the week to simplify shopping.
  • ✓ Speed: All recipes are fast. You can pre-boil eggs for the Cottage Cheese bowl to make it a 1-minute meal.

The Recipes

1. Soy Sauce & Mushroom Omelet

High Protein

A savory, umami-packed omelet. Serve with avocado on the side.

Ingredients

  • 3 whole eggs
  • 1 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 green onion, sliced
  • 1/2 avocado (on the side)
  • 1 tsp cooking oil/butter

Instructions

  1. Sauté mushrooms in a hot pan (3-4 mins).
  2. Whisk eggs and soy sauce.
  3. Add eggs and green onions to the pan.
  4. Cook gently, fold, and serve with avocado.

2. Almond Butter Protein Yogurt

Fastest (2 Mins)

A protein, fat, and fiber-packed bowl that keeps you full all morning.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (220g) Non-Fat Greek Yogurt
  • 1 scoop Vanilla Whey Protein
  • 1 tbsp natural almond butter
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1/4 cup berries

Instructions

  1. Mix yogurt and protein powder until smooth.
  2. Stir in almond butter and chia seeds.
  3. Top with berries.
  4. (Optional: Add 1 tsp water if too thick).

3. Chili Crisp Cottage Cheese

1-Min Prep

The fastest option. Prep hard-boiled eggs ahead of time for the week.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (220g) Cottage Cheese
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, halved
  • 1 tbsp chili crisp
  • 1 green onion, sliced
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Add cottage cheese to a bowl.
  2. Top with hard-boiled eggs.
  3. Drizzle with chili crisp.
  4. Garnish with green onion and sesame seeds.

4. Peanut Butter Banana Protein Oats

Make-Ahead Option

Warm, comforting oats with massive protein. Can be made as overnight oats or cooked fresh.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 scoop Vanilla Whey Protein
  • 1 tbsp natural peanut butter
  • 1 medium banana, sliced
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Cook oats with almond milk and salt (5 mins).
  2. Remove from heat. Let cool 1-2 mins.
  3. Stir in protein powder until smooth.
  4. Mix in peanut butter and half the banana.
  5. Top with remaining banana and cinnamon.
  6. Overnight option: Mix all ingredients, refrigerate overnight.
